About me

I'm interested in tech roles that improve humanity and our earth, including AI for social good, computational journalism, and innovative software engineering roles that tackle real-world problems. I study Computer Science and Digital Humanities (minor) at Stanford University. My studies and projects focuses on AI and Information and a bit of Human Computer Interaction. Projects

  • Research

    Summer 2023

    #Presstitute: Online Violence Faced By Filipina Journalists and Fighting Back with Data

    I developed an interactive article on with scrollytelling and data visualizations.   • Coursework

    Spring 2023

    Campus Crimewatch

    For the Building News Applications Spring 2023 course, we created a website aiming to create a centralized, accessible picture of crime and safety on college campuses. We also created a pipeline and extensive documentation for college campuses to replicate this. I collected, wrangled, and geocoded crime data using ArcGIS API that was later visualized crimes into an interactive map. I also produced crime stats from data analysis using D3.js, and developed an automated news ticker using an RSS feed and GitHub actions.

  • Research

    Spring 2023

    Data From Docs - The Brown Institute Magic Grant Awardee

    In 2023 late spring and early fall, I helped in the grant proposal and start of Data From Docs, a 2023-2024 Magic Grant Recipient from The Brown Institute for Media Innovation (partnership between Columbia Journalism School and Stanford’s School of Engineering). The project aims to create an accessible suite of tools (OCR, entity recognition, data extraction using LLMs etc.) to help journalists, local especially, discover stories in large, unstructured sets of documents. The project tests and builds upon existing tools (such as Document Cloud and other BigLocal tools), with police misconduct data and local governing bodies' minutes collected by AgendaWatch as its evaluation dataset.

  • Research

    Winter 2022 - Spring 2023

    AI Literacy Project

    I took on multiple roles in the ongoing AI literacy project, currently called Classroom-Ready Resources About AI For Teaching (CRAFT). I created the entire curriculum for the AI & Math Unit including teaching resources. I developed 2 interactive tools (Datamax and Analyze), and the Resources tab of explorable AI literacy resources using technologies including Typescript, GoogleAPI, and Observable. I also facilitated classroom sessions, and conducted multiple co-design and user testing interviews on our curriculum and CRAFT website. This project was presented at the CHI AI literacy workshop and appeared in 4 papers, one of which I am cited as author.

  • Coursework

    Winter 2022

    Section Leader Helper

    For CS193x: Web Programming Fundamentals, my final project was a multi-purpose tool to help with Section Leaders (SLs) as I was a section leader at the time. This includes (1) a teaching tool to create cards of their practice whiteboard, links to handout/solutions and (2) a grading tool to store comments reused for different submissions that can be viewed per homework assignment. It is made entirely from scratch, with the frontend written in HTML and CSS and Javascript, and the backend written in Javascript, specifically Node and Express, and uses a MongoDB database.
